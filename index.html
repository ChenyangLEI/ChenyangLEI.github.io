Chenyang Lei is currently the Technical Lead for "Unified Multimodal Understanding and Generation" at Huawei's Leibniz Institute, Celia Lab. He is also the technical owner of the Celia Image Editor (小艺修图), a key feature for the Mate 80 Pro. He was an assistant professor at the Centre for Artificial Intelligence and Robotics (CAIR), Chinese Academy of Sciences, and a visiting scholar at Princeton University working with Felix Heide. He received his Ph.D. in computer science from the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST), supervised by Qifeng Chen. He was a research intern at MSRA, NVIDIA, and SenseTime, and obtained his Bachelor's degree at Zhejiang University in 2018.

Research

I am interested in exploring system designs of visual computing pipelines, from advanced sensing technologies to more capable artificial intelligence. My current research topics include:

  • Omni-Modality Beyond audio, vision, and language — toward a unified treatment of all physical signals.
  • Unified Multimodal Understanding and Generation Building a single system that both understands and generates.
  • AI-Native Hardware Designing everything — mechanical structure, sensors, and the product itself — for AI from the ground up.
